Case Study: Greener Routes to Adipic Acid

Adipic acid is a key monomer for the production of Nylon 6,6, produced on a massive scale (billions of kilograms annually). The traditional industrial process involves the oxidation of a cyclohexane derivative using concentrated nitric acid.

While effective, this process generates significant quantities of nitrous oxide (N2O), a potent greenhouse gas and ozone-depleting substance. This has driven extensive research into greener alternatives.

One leading approach, championed by companies and academic groups, involves using glucose (derived from renewable biomass) as a starting material. Through a series of biocatalytic and chemocatalytic steps, glucose can be converted to adipic acid without using nitric acid and without producing N2O.

This case study perfectly illustrates the green chemistry principles of using renewable feedstocks and designing safer chemistry to prevent pollution at its source.
