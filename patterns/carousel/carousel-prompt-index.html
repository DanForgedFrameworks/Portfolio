What Can the Distomer Do?

Technical principles of chirality and its biological implications.

Inactive Ballast

The distomer may provide no therapeutic benefit while still placing a metabolic burden on the body.

In Ibuprofen, (S)-ibuprofen acts as the active pain reliever, while (R)-ibuprofen is slowly converted in the body to the active form.

Requires analysis of metabolic delay and the liver's capacity to handle inactive ballast conversion.

Different Biological Effect

The unwanted enantiomer can possess an entirely different therapeutic value.

One enantiomer of Propoxyphene acts as an analgesic, while the other functions as an antitussive for cough suppression.

Necessitates discrete clinical trials and regulatory approval for each enantiomer as separate drug products.

Toxic Effects

Thalidomide represents the most critical example of enantiomeric toxicity.

The R-enantiomer is a sedative, while the S-enantiomer is teratogenic; crucially, the forms interconvert in the body.

In vivo interconversion means isolated enantiomer administration cannot mitigate toxicity risks.
